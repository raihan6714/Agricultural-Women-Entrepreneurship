What we do

Agricultural Women Entrepreneurship Impact Investment Hub is built on 3 pillars. Women agri-entrepreneurs can access our services under each of the pillars

PILLAR I – Technical Assistance

Technical assistance, coaching, mentoring of agri-entrepreneurs so they may grow their agri-businesses access new markets and reach bankable status

PILLAR II - FINANCE

Linking agri-businesses with the finance community, social impact funds, through which women-agri- entrepreneurs can develop and upscale their businesses

PILLAR III – TECHNOLOGY

Agri-business , engaging the private sector in the drive to bring new agri-equipment, food processing technology, packaging to the agri-value and food supply chains in Africa

Our Network

The Agricultural Women Entrepreneurship Impact Investment Hub is a network of

International and African professionals supporting women agri-entrepreneurs in Africa and Small Island Developing States. We have experts across 30 African countries.

Agricultural Women Entrepreneurship Businesses

Our primary beneficiaries are agri-businesses

that are at least 51% woman-owned, woman-led, and in an agri- value chain traditionally dominated by women producers.

Our mission

To Assist less privilege women and the youth in the urban communities.

RELAY

Agricultural Women Entrepreneurship Impact Investment Hub aims to inspire, disrupt and connect by bringing together women agri- entrepreneurs innovators and business angel investors, philanthropists and public donors and connect by bringing together.

SMART ECONOMICS

Together we are committed to promoting Gender Smart Investment in Agriculture & Agri Value Chains. Women are present throughout the agri-value chains from farm to fork. If we want sustainable agri-supply chains, investing in women is simply SMART ECONOMICS !

ECONOMIC GROWTH

Agricultural Women Entrepreneurship members share the conviction that it is the time to shine the light on women’s actual contribution to agr- supply chains and services by investing in them, sot hey may reach their full potential and contribute actively to ECONOMIC GROWTH AND JOB CREATION

Social Impact through Women

All Agricultural Women Entrepreneurship entrepreneurs commit and comply

with the Agricultural Women Entrepreneurship Social Impact Charter. Their commitment helps to protect and advance the socio-economic interests of women in their supply chain, their agri-businesses and communities.

Agricultural Women Entrepreneurship Today

At present the COVID19 pandemic is having a direct impact on our women led agri-sme's and on rural livelihoods across Africa and Small Island Developing States. Market linkages are particularly at risk, as food systems already fragile due to over dependency on imports must adapt to disruptions in the agri- value chain and changing consumer demand. Women are the links in these local and regional agri- value chains and the most vulnerable to the economic and sanitary crisis. In our #CounteringCOVID19 campaign, Agricultural Women Entrepreneurship is shining a light on our women agri-entrepreneurs, their trojan work and contributions to combatting the health and economic crisis by protecting their workers and maintaining safe food supply for the rural and urban populations.

Agricultural Women Entrepreneurship Tomorrow

Our aim is to support women to progress to the next growth level wherever they are situated in the agri-value chain. For them to graduate from subsistence to self-reliancy with viable agri-businesses. Our innovative approach is open to women in producer groups, cooperatives, agri-sme's. Growth is only possible with access to knowledge and resources to meet market demands. Our tailored mentoring helps women in their business growth cycle by providing them with access to technical assistance, patient capital, better technology from production to packaging of their products. We want our women agri-entrepreneurs to continue to be the drivers of social, economic and environmental advancement. We want them to become visible market leaders, to inspire the next generation of Agricultural Women Entrepreneurship entrepeneurs.
